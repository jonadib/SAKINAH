A Tribute to Osman Hadi

Who was Osman Hadi?

Sharif Osman bin Hadi (born June 30, 1993, Nalchhiti, Jhalokathi) was a politically conscious educator, social worker, and an uncompromising revolutionary voice. Raised in a religious environment, he studied Political Science at the University of Dhaka and later joined the teaching profession. During the July 2024 mass uprising, he emerged as a frontline organizer and coordinator, providing leadership at a critical moment. After the fall of Sheikh Hasina, he came into prominence as the co-founder and spokesperson of the Inqilab Manch. His voice was clear and unwavering on issues such as justice for the July martyrs, ending impunity, political accountability, and resistance against state authoritarianism. He believed that a state survives through justice—not through fear or servitude. On December 12, 2025, he was shot in a planned armed attack in Paltan, Dhaka, and on December 18 he attained martyrdom at Singapore General Hospital. At just 32, his body fell silent, but the questions he raised, the struggles he waged, and the consciousness he ignited did not. Sharif Osman bin Hadi is no longer just a name; he is a legacy—one that continues to compel a new generation to stand up.
